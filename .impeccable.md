## Design Context

### Users
- Primary product: Consulting OS for consulting offices and their clients.
- Core user groups: Super Admin, Office Owner/Manager/Employee, Client Owner/Employee.
- Usage context: day-to-day operational work such as project tracking, client collaboration, team coordination, and billing visibility.
- Core job to be done: help offices run reliably, communicate clearly with clients, and maintain execution control across workflows.

### Brand Personality
- Personality words: Trustworthy, Focused, Empowering.
- Voice and tone: precise, calm, professional, and action-oriented.
- Emotional goals: build confidence in decisions, reduce operational ambiguity, and support users in taking clear next actions.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Canonical frontend/design system source: `front-end-consult-angular21-code`.
- Visual references: Asana, Trello, Jira (clarity of structure, scannable task states, and productivity-first layouts).
- Theme strategy: equal parity between light and dark modes.
- Accessibility baseline: color contrast and keyboard navigation as non-negotiable defaults.
- Established implementation cues from current Angular system:
  - Deep navy/surface-first UI with sky/cyan primary and green accent.
  - Medium radii, clear focus rings, restrained motion, and RTL-safe spacing.
  - Typography optimized for bilingual use (Arabic/English).

### Design Principles
1. Operational Clarity First: prioritize scannable hierarchy, explicit states, and obvious action paths over decorative complexity.
2. Confidence Through Consistency: reuse shared tokens, spacing, and interaction patterns to make behavior predictable across pages.
3. Accessibility by Default: enforce strong contrast, visible focus, full keyboard support, and reduced-motion-friendly interactions.
4. Empower Through Feedback: surface meaningful status, validation, and progress signals so users can act without hesitation.
5. Bilingual-Ready Systems: keep layouts and components RTL-safe and content structures equally robust in Arabic and English.
